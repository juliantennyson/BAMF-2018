What to Expect

Expect a fun and responsible experience in a fantastical wonderland known as the Lookout Arts Quarry. We have 6 active stages full of music, stand up and improv comedy, workshops, yoga, flow artists, puppet shows, karaoke, plus an art gallery, art installations, a tea lounge, and more!

These activities run at all hours of daylight and starlight. Please arrive well-rested and equipped to take care of yourself for the whole weekend.

Things to Know

The festival will run from Friday, May 31 at 3pm to Sunday, June 2 at 5pm.

All biological functions can be handled onsite, from water access to food access to toilets!

Water:
Can be found at several spigots around the site. Lit up in blue at night for your convenience.

Food:
Food vendors will operate alongside each other and even take the early morning and late night shifts to keep all you dancing fools fueled for the full fest

Toilets:
A healthy mix of blue goo and composting toilets can be found all around the site
The composting toilets help the Quarry build up their stores of compostable waste. Do your business, sprinkle some saw dust on top, and voila! You’ve just made the earth a greener place.

Zero Waste

BAMF! is committed to making small improvements year-after-year to make our festival as green as can be. But we can’t do it without you! Here are some ideas:

  • Please use the many, many trash cans onsite to sort your recycling, food waste, and trash into the proper receptacle. Bring reusable utensils and dishware.
  • Pack it in, pack it out: thinking of the Quarry like you would a pristine nature reserve. The Quarriors who reside out here call this place home and worked hard to restore its natural environment. Let’s help them stay true to their goal and pack out everything you pack in.
  • The devil is in the details: keep track of receipts, earplugs, gum wrappers, and cigarette butts. Pack them out or have a Zero Waste volunteer help you sort them into the right receptacle.

Weather

The Northwest can be a fickle beast, so please remember that nighttime will be very cold! Bring layers and blankets to keep yourself swaddled with. Bring closed-toed shoes to protect from the elements. Bellingham Arts & Music Festival can quickly become Bellingham Arts & Mud Festival! See our packing list.

Safety

We want to be real with you: the Quarry can be a dangerous place. This section is devoted to the safety services we have ramped up for 2019 and explains a bit more about the Quarry property.

  • Medical
    • The Medical tent is located between Cedar Stage and the Quarry water, next to the outdoor shower
    • Medical personnel are on-call throughout the weekend and can be spotted via their bright red shirts
    • We will call 911 for Emergency Medical Services if our staff and our medical team deem this to be necessary
      • The closest hospital is Skagit Valley Hospital, 18.3 miles or a 20-minute drive away.
  • Starfl33t
    • This harm reduction team is dedicated to the health and well-being of all attendees, particularly their mental health
      • Harm Reduction are preventative services implemented to reduce potential risks. The services Starfl33t offers include a chill-out zone, snacks and beverages, ramping all the way to psychedelic first aid.
    • The Starfl33t tent will be located a stone’s throw from the Medical tent, across from Quarry stage
    • We encourage you to say hi and welcome Starfl33t to the team!
  • Peacekeepers
    • An intermediary team between attendees and security, the Peacekeepers are a bystander intervention and harm reduction team. They can be identified by their bee-themed hats and attire.
      • This team is designed to intervene in situations where a member of the crowd is making others uncomfortable and to check on people in the crowd who may need some extra support.
    • Reach out to them for earplugs, water, fun surprises, for a compassionate ear, or for help.
  • Security and SEAT
    • Security is a de-escalation-trained team that exists to keep the crowd safe. They will wear bright green t-shirts.
    • More info on SEAT coming soon

All of our Safety personnel work together to create a comprehensive, preventative-based culture of health and safety at this event.

Please do not hesitate to speak to any of them should you need assistance of any kind!

The Quarry

We are so honored to be able to host BAMF! at this incredible venue. Did you know the Lookout Arts Quarry operates all year long? Did you know that people live out here? Did you know most of them are craftspeople and world-renowned circus performers?
There’s more than meets the eye on this beautiful site and that includes lots of fully or partially completed projects created by the artists who live there. If you see something interesting, but it doesn’t look like it’s part of BAMF!, it probably isn’t! Please leave the art fixtures and makeshift shelters be. This helps keep the property intact and our patrons safe. We are visitors to people’s homes when we come for BAMF! We all like to host courteous guests—pay it forward ☺ For more information, please check out their website: https://lookoutarts.com/

Parking and Camping

All parking will begin at 3pm on Friday, May 31st, when the Box Office opens. PLEASE do not park on Hwy 99 or your car will be towed.
Our friends at the Quarry have worked hard to open up MORE parking spots—meaning we get to see MORE of YOU! Read below for changes to parking:

There are 3 lot options:

  • Front Lot
    • $30
    • Can come and go as needed
    • Not near camping
  • Camping Loop
    • $40
    • Prime area next to the forested or grassy campground areas
    • Parked in for the weekend. Access restricted after 7pm both nights.
  • Easement
    • $20
    • A bit off the beaten path
    • Parked in for the weekend. Access restricted after 7pm both nights.

Save money with our “Clown Car” discount! If each of your car seats are full of *people* your parking is FREE for ANY lot. Carpool! Check back soon for a Facebook rideshare page.

Front Lot parkers will have to haul their gear up to the campground. The Campground has wooded, shaded areas and a grassy area called the Prairie. Cars are not allowed on the Prairie.

We prioritize disability accommodations on the Prairie. Please contact us at bamf.bham@gmail.com for disability accommodations.

What to Bring

  • Sleeping bag and sleeping pad
  • Tent and rain fly
  • Hammock
  • Food or cash for vendors
  • Awareness of your body and the bodies of others and their personal bubbles
  • Reusable water bottle and mug
  • Costumes! Keep your face visible, please
  • Flashlight/headlamp
  • External phone battery or power bank
  • Raincoat and lost of warm layers
  • Flow toys
  • Closed-toe shoes are recommended (required to volunteer)
  • Back up pair of shoes
  • Eco-friendly glitter
  • Sunscreen

What Not to Bring

  • Glass
  • Dogs and other pets. Service animals allowed under these conditions:
    • Paperwork present
    • Leashed at all times
    • ESA/SA Vest or other marker at all times
  • Outside alcohol
  • Drugs
  • Propane stoves, camping stoves, JetBoils, or open flames
  • Weapons of any kind
  • Exclusivity or violence - The Quarry has a zero tolerance policy toward violence of any kind and we fully support that aim. We want this space to be inclusive and supportive, let’s build each other up and keep everyone safe.
